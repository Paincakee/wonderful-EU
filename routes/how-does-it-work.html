How does it work??

At the heart of the EU's functioning lies a delicate balance of institutions and mechanisms designed to promote cooperation and decision-making among member states. The key institutions include:

The European Commission: The executive branch of the EU responsible for proposing legislation, implementing decisions, and upholding the EU's treaties.

The European Parliament: The legislative body directly elected by EU citizens, representing their interests and playing a crucial role in shaping EU policies.

The Council of the European Union: Comprised of government ministers from each member state, the Council shares legislative and decision-making authority with the European Parliament.

The European Council: Consisting of the heads of state or government of EU member states, the European Council provides strategic direction and sets the EU's political agenda.

The Court of Justice of the European Union: Ensures the uniform interpretation and application of EU law, safeguarding the rights and interests of EU citizens.

Through a system of checks and balances, these institutions work together to formulate policies, enact legislation, and address the challenges facing Europe and its citizens.
